## Overview

Weapons in Conspiracy Game are not primarily damage-dealing tools. They are **information and influence modifiers** used to alter player interactions, credibility, and outcomes of encounters.

Weapons are:

* Limited-use
* Context-sensitive
* Often ambiguous in effect (can create doubt)

---

## Weapon Categories

### 1. Lethal Weapons

Used to eliminate or attempt elimination of players.

Examples:

* Pistol
* Poison vial
* Hidden blade

Properties:

* May fail or be redirected
* May leave evidence
* Often require conditions (proximity, trust, timing)

Design Notes:

* Killing is risky and should generate information
* Failed attempts are as valuable as successful ones

---

### 2. Covert Weapons

Used to manipulate without obvious detection.

Examples:

* Blackmail dossier
* Forged documents
* Bribe funds

Effects:

* Force actions
* Alter votes
* Silence targets

Design Notes:

* Core to conspiracy gameplay
* Should create second-order effects (paranoia, suspicion)

---

### 3. Informational Weapons

Used to reveal or distort truth.

Examples:

* Surveillance report
* Intercepted letter
* Spy network

Effects:

* Reveal partial roles
* Confirm or deny alibis
* Introduce false positives

Design Notes:

* Information should never be absolute
* High-value but uncertain

---

### 4. Social Weapons

Operate through reputation and trust.

Examples:

* Public accusation
* Reputation token
* Royal decree

Effects:

* Shift group perception
* Increase/decrease credibility
* Influence group decisions

Design Notes:

* No hard mechanics → emergent gameplay

---

## Core Mechanics

### Evidence Generation

Every weapon interaction may produce:

* Evidence (true)
* False evidence
* Missing evidence

### Attribution

Actions may be:

* Known
* Hidden
* Misattributed

### Risk vs Reward

* Strong effects → higher exposure
* Safe actions → weaker impact

---

## Balancing Principles

* No guaranteed outcomes
* Information > raw power
* Encourage interaction between players

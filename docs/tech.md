# Conspiracy — Technology System Design Document

*"Civilisations do not advance. They maintain, or they decay. The distinction is administrative."*

**Game:** Conspiracy: A Simulation of Power, Paranoia, and Poor Decision-Making
**Author:** Alexei Fedotov

---

## 1. Overview

Technology in *Conspiracy* is not a tree of discrete unlocks. It is a continuous scalar — a measure of how well a Dominion maintains, develops, and applies knowledge in a given direction.

There is no "researching" a technology. There is only **investment** (keeping the level up) and **neglect** (letting it fall). Levels degrade without ongoing effort. Wars accelerate that degradation. Adjacent regions and related directions spread their levels passively. A Dominion that stops paying attention wakes up three turns later to find it has fallen behind.

**Key design intent:** Technology should feel like infrastructure — invisible when functioning, catastrophic when it fails. The player's job is not to "unlock" things but to maintain a portfolio of capabilities against constant decay pressure.

---

## 2. Technology Directions

There are seven directions. Each is tracked as an independent level per Dominion, but also per region where regional variance matters (see §4.3).

| Direction | Covers | Primary input | Key cross-dependencies |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cognitive Systems** | Computation, administration automation, AI, surveillance | Scholarly Output | Industrial Engineering, Ideological Technology |
| **Life Sciences** | Medicine, genetics, biotech, longevity | Scholarly Output, Belief | Cognitive Systems, Ideological Technology |
| **Industrial Engineering** | Manufacturing, materials, energy production, construction | Industrial Output | Cognitive Systems, Maritime, Aerospace |
| **Maritime Operations** | Ocean infrastructure, underwater habitats, naval logistics | Industrial Output | Industrial Engineering, Aerospace |
| **Aerospace** | Orbital infrastructure, space logistics, remote sensing | Industrial Output, Scholarly Output | Industrial Engineering, Military Doctrine |
| **Ideological Technology** | Propaganda, belief engineering, social control, cult mechanics | Belief, Influence | Cognitive Systems, Life Sciences |
| **Military Doctrine** | Weapons, logistics, tactics, army organisation | Industrial Output, Trust | Industrial Engineering, Cognitive Systems |

Each direction has a **level** ranging from 0.0 to 10.0. Levels are stored as floats in `world/tech.json` per Dominion.

---

## 3. Level Update Formula

Each turn, the simulation updates every tech direction level using:

```
Δlevel = investment_gain
       + diffusion_gain
       + synergy_gain
       − decay
       − crisis_loss
```

All five components are computed independently and summed. The result is clamped to [0.0, 10.0].

---

### 3.1 Investment Gain

The player allocates **Research Points** (generated by Scholarly Output) across directions each turn via the Orders panel. Unallocated Research Points are lost.

```
investment_gain = allocated_points × education_modifier × diminishing_return_factor
```

- `education_modifier` — derived from the Bureaucrat and Scholar strata education level in the Dominion's core regions. Range: 0.5 (very low education) to 2.0 (highly educated).
- `diminishing_return_factor = 1 / (1 + current_level × 0.15)` — advancing from 7 to 8 requires roughly 2× the investment of advancing from 1 to 2.

**Minimum investment to hold a level (break-even):**

| Level range | Research Points/turn to prevent decay |
|---|---|
| 0–2 | 1 |
| 3–4 | 2 |
| 5–6 | 4 |
| 7–8 | 7 |
| 9–10 | 12 |

A Dominion that invests exactly at break-even holds its level. Below break-even, decay begins.

---

### 3.2 Diffusion Gain

Tech levels spread passively from adjacent regions and from allied/trading Dominions.

```
diffusion_gain = Σ max(0, source_level − current_level) × diffusion_coefficient
```

- Diffusion only flows **downhill** — a lower-level Dominion benefits from a higher-level neighbour; the reverse does not apply.
- `diffusion_coefficient` by relationship type:

| Relationship | Coefficient |
|---|---|
| Active trade route with adjacent region | 0.04 per source |
| Alliance | 0.03 per source |
| Neutral / no trade | 0.01 per source |
| At war | 0.00 (tech diffusion blocked) |
| Occupied territory (controlled) | 0.06 — the occupier extracts knowledge faster |

**Design implication:** Isolationist Dominions fall behind. The best way to maintain high-tier tech in a neglected direction is to trade with a neighbour who invests in it. The second-best way is to occupy them.

---

### 3.3 Synergy Gain

Related directions passively boost each other at a small rate.

```
synergy_gain = Σ (related_direction_level × synergy_coefficient)
```

Synergy coefficients per direction pair:

| Direction A | Direction B | Coefficient |
|---|---|---|
| Cognitive Systems | Industrial Engineering | 0.02 |
| Cognitive Systems | Ideological Technology | 0.03 |
| Cognitive Systems | Military Doctrine | 0.02 |
| Life Sciences | Ideological Technology | 0.02 |
| Life Sciences | Military Doctrine | 0.01 |
| Industrial Engineering | Maritime Operations | 0.02 |
| Industrial Engineering | Aerospace | 0.02 |
| Industrial Engineering | Military Doctrine | 0.03 |
| Maritime Operations | Aerospace | 0.01 |
| Aerospace | Military Doctrine | 0.02 |
| Ideological Technology | Military Doctrine | 0.02 |

Synergy is small per turn but compounds over time. A Dominion that develops Industrial Engineering and Cognitive Systems together will find Military Doctrine drifting upward even without direct investment.

---

### 3.4 Decay

All tech directions decay each turn. Decay is reduced (but never eliminated) by investment.

```
decay = base_decay_rate × max(0, 1 − investment_ratio)
```

- `base_decay_rate` = 0.05 per turn at all levels — slow, but meaningful over 20+ turns.
- `investment_ratio = allocated_points / break_even_points` — if you're investing at 50% of break-even, decay is 50% of base rate.
- Full investment fully suppresses decay. Partial investment partially suppresses it.

**Education modifier on decay:** High-education populations preserve knowledge better. `education_modifier` reduces decay rate by up to 40% at maximum education.

**Institutional knowledge:** Dominions with a large Bureaucrat stratum decay more slowly in Cognitive Systems and Ideological Technology (the bureaucracy preserves procedure even without active investment). This is passive and cannot be turned off.

---

### 3.5 Crisis Loss

Military action and catastrophic events cause immediate, sharp drops in tech level.

```
crisis_loss = event_severity × direction_vulnerability
```

**Military conflict:**

| Conflict type | Loss per affected turn | Directions affected |
|---|---|---|
| Active war in core region | −0.3 to −0.8 | Industrial Engineering, Life Sciences, Cognitive Systems (labs destroyed, scholars flee) |
| Occupation of a core region | −1.0 to −2.0 (one-time) | All directions — occupation disrupts institutional knowledge |
| Prolonged siege | −0.2 per turn | Industrial Engineering, Military Doctrine |
| Naval blockade | −0.1 per turn | Maritime Operations, Industrial Engineering (import-dependent) |

**Events (examples):**

| Event | Tech loss |
|---|---|
| Scholar exodus (loyalty crisis) | Cognitive Systems −0.5; Life Sciences −0.3 |
| Cult uprising suppressed | Ideological Technology −0.4 |
| Factory collapse | Industrial Engineering −0.3 |
| Orbital station destroyed | Aerospace −1.5 |
| Propaganda failure (belief collapse) | Ideological Technology −0.6 |
| Plague | Life Sciences −0.2; population education modifier reduced for 3 turns |
| "The Ministry of Research has lost the files" | Any direction −0.2 (bureaucratic accident; random) |

**Design implication:** War is not just a military problem — it is a technological one. A long campaign in core regions can set a Dominion back years of accumulated research. The player must weigh military ambition against the research cost.

## 4. Level Effects

Tech levels produce continuous modifiers, not discrete unlocks. Effects scale with level.

### 4.1 Continuous Modifiers

| Direction | Level × modifier | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Cognitive Systems | × 3% | `paperwork_overflow_threshold` increase |
| Cognitive Systems | × 2% | `faction_ai_prediction_accuracy` increase |
| Life Sciences | × 4% | Hero trait mutation rate decrease (heroes decay slower) |
| Life Sciences | × 2% | Population health events frequency decrease |
| Industrial Engineering | × 4% | `industrial_output` increase |
| Industrial Engineering | × 3% | `construction_cost` decrease |
| Maritime Operations | × 5% | `naval_capacity` increase |
| Maritime Operations | × 3% | `coastal_resource_output` increase |
| Aerospace | × 4% | `rival_visibility` increase |
| Aerospace | × 3% | `trade_route_range` increase |
| Ideological Technology | × 4% | `propaganda_effectiveness` increase |
| Ideological Technology | × 3% | `belief_drift_resistance` increase |
| Military Doctrine | × 4% | `army_strength` effective multiplier |
| Military Doctrine | × 3% | `supply_chain_efficiency` increase |

All modifiers are multiplicative against base values.

### 4.2 Threshold Effects

At integer thresholds, additional capabilities unlock:

| Direction | Level | Threshold effect |
|---|---|---|
| Cognitive Systems | 3 | Policy available: Automated Clerks (Paperwork cap +30%) |
| Cognitive Systems | 5 | Policy available: Predictive Governance (stability events: +2 turn warning) |
| Cognitive Systems | 8 | Region type unlocked: Digital Enclave |
| Life Sciences | 3 | Hero recruitment: choose 1 starting trait |
| Life Sciences | 5 | Event table removed: Scholar Plague (natural disease suppressed) |
| Life Sciences | 8 | Policy available: Extended Tenure (hero aging halted) |
| Industrial Engineering | 3 | Building unlocked: Advanced Manufactory |
| Industrial Engineering | 5 | Policy available: Industrial Drive (+20% output, +5 unrest/turn) |
| Industrial Engineering | 8 | Building unlocked: Fusion Reactor (requires Energy investment) |
| Maritime Operations | 3 | Region type available: Coastal Extraction Zone |
| Maritime Operations | 6 | Region type available: Ocean Subrealm |
| Aerospace | 4 | Building unlocked: Orbital Station |
| Aerospace | 7 | Region type available: Off-World Colony |
| Ideological Technology | 3 | Mission available: Precision Propaganda (targets specific stratum) |
| Ideological Technology | 5 | Policy available: Belief Stabilisation (halts Belief drift in one region) |
| Ideological Technology | 8 | Mission available: Found State Religion (formalises dominant belief) |
| Military Doctrine | 3 | Unit type unlocked: Professional Corps |
| Military Doctrine | 5 | Order type unlocked: Strategic Encirclement |
| Military Doctrine | 8 | Unit type unlocked: Mechanised Division |

Thresholds are **not permanent** — if a level drops below the threshold, the associated capability is suspended (not lost forever, but unavailable until the level recovers).

### 4.3 Regional Variance

Tech levels are tracked at the Dominion level by default. However, regions can diverge from the Dominion average:

- **Core Homeland** — matches Dominion average exactly.
- **Colony** — lags the Dominion average by up to −1.5 in all directions (distance from research centres).
- **Occupied Territory** — degrades at 2× base decay rate (institutional knowledge drains under occupation).
- **Subrealm** — separate tech levels entirely; runs on Archival Trust economy; may exceed surface levels in Industrial Engineering and Life Sciences due to historical depth.

Regional variance matters for adjacency diffusion — a high-tech colony in a low-tech region can export tech to that region even if the Dominion core is far away.

## 5. The Education Multiplier

Education is the most important long-term lever on the tech system. It affects investment gain, decay resistance, and diffusion absorption rate.

**Education level** is tracked per stratum per region. The Dominion's effective `education_modifier` is a weighted average across the Scholar and Bureaucrat strata in core regions.

| Population condition | Education modifier effect |
|---|---|
| Scholar stratum large and loyal | +0.4 to investment gain |
| Bureaucrat stratum large | −0.2 to decay rate (institutional preservation) |
| Commons stratum poorly educated | −0.2 to investment gain (knowledge doesn't propagate) |
| Cult stratum > 30% population | −0.1 to Cognitive Systems investment gain (anti-rationalist pressure) |
| Scholar exodus event | education_modifier −0.5 for 3 turns |
| University building present | +0.3 to regional education modifier |

**Design implication:** Tech investment without education investment is inefficient. A Dominion that builds its Scholar stratum earns compounding returns on every Research Point spent. A Dominion that neglects education pays full price for diminishing returns.

---

## 6. Investment Strategy

Players allocate Research Points in the Orders panel each turn. The allocation is:

- **Per-direction** (how many points to each of the seven directions)
- **Flexible each turn** — there is no commitment; the player can shift allocation freely
- **Bounded by Scholarly Output** — total allocatable points = total Scholarly Output this turn

**Recommended mental model for the player:** Think of Research Points as a maintenance budget. First priority is holding existing levels at break-even. Second priority is advancing the directions that matter for current strategic goals. Everything else decays.

A Dominion that tries to advance all seven directions simultaneously will fail to hold any of them. Specialisation is viable. Total breadth is not.

---

## 7. Inter-Dominion Technology Gaps

The tech gap between the Dominion and rivals is a strategic variable in its own right.

| Gap | Effect |
|---|---|
| Rival is +2 or more in Military Doctrine | Rival armies receive effective strength bonus in engagements against the Dominion |
| Rival is +2 or more in Cognitive Systems | Rival covert missions against the Dominion: +15% success rate |
| Rival is +2 or more in Ideological Technology | Rival propaganda operations in the Dominion's regions: +20% effectiveness |
| Dominion is +3 or more in any direction vs. adjacent region | Diffusion export begins — the Dominion passively raises adjacent region's level (small effect; useful diplomatically) |

Tech gaps are visible to the player via the Statistics panel (direction levels graphed per Dominion over time). Rivals' tech levels are partially visible — exact values require a Cognitive Systems level ≥ 5 or an active espionage mission.

---

## 8. Design Principles

- **Maintenance, not advancement.** The primary skill is sustaining levels under pressure, not racing to unlock capabilities. Players who treat tech as a "get it and forget it" system will find their levels quietly eroding.
- **War has a research bill.** Military campaigns that pass through core regions degrade the directions that make the next campaign possible. The cost of war is not just Trust and soldiers — it's the research that didn't happen.
- **Education compounds.** Investment in the Scholar and Bureaucrat strata is the highest-return long-term tech action in the game. It doesn't show up immediately. It shows up twenty turns later when every Research Point is worth 1.8× what it was.
- **Specialisation is viable; breadth is expensive.** A Dominion that focuses on two or three directions and maintains them at high levels is stronger than one that spreads investment thinly across all seven.
- **Diffusion creates interdependence.** Neighbours matter. Being next to a high-Cognitive-Systems Dominion is worth passive investment in that direction. Cutting off trade routes to isolate a rival also cuts off incoming diffusion.
- **Levels are legible.** The player should be able to look at their tech levels and read the story of the last twenty turns — the war that knocked Industrial Engineering down, the education investment that slowly pushed Cognitive Systems up, the neglected Maritime direction drifting toward zero.

# Conspiracy — Regions System Design Document

*"Every region is a failed state in progress. Some are simply further along."*

**Game:** Conspiracy: A Simulation of Power, Paranoia, and Poor Decision-Making
**Author:** Alexei Fedotov

---

> **Architecture note:** Regions are defined in `world/regions/` as JSON objects in the player's fork. The Python simulation engine evaluates region state each turn (economy, belief drift, entropy accumulation, event rolls). The browser client renders region data read directly from world-state JSON — no region logic runs client-side.

---

# 1. REGION OVERVIEW

## 1.1 What a Region Is

A **Region** is a semi-autonomous administrative unit pretending to be a coherent place. It has:

- A governing **Ideology** (which faction's worldview currently dominates)
- A **Belief Composition** (what the population actually believes, which drifts independently of governance)
- A **Stability Index** (how successfully the region pretends that everything is fine)
- An **Entropy Accumulation** (how much systemic pressure is building toward the next crisis)
- A set of **Resources** (material and ideological)
- A **Population** composed of social strata with their own loyalties and vices

Regions are macro-level units. They can contain sub-regions (provinces), but the simulation tracks them as a single entity unless a province-level event fires.

## 1.2 Region Categories

All regions belong to one of three political archetypes, inherited from the dominant faction type:

| Archetype | Description | Primary resource | Primary failure mode |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Federation of Belief** | Citizens vote for their gods; consensus drives everything | Belief | Theological schism; electoral miracle fraud |
| **Syndicate of Necessity** | Corporations worship efficiency; everything is a transaction | Trust | Regulatory capture; efficiency cults |
| **Realm of Regret** | Monarchs rule out of habit; legitimacy is mostly aesthetic | Influence | Dynastic instability; nostalgia riots |

A region's archetype shapes which events fire, which missions are available, and how the population responds to ideology changes.

# 2. RESOURCE MODEL

## 2.1 Primary Resources (Dominion-Level)

These are the main strategic currencies tracked on the Top Bar. Regions produce, consume, and transmit them.

| Resource | Generated by | Consumed by | Collapse effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Trust** | Public promises; propaganda; stability | Decrees; diplomacy; missions | Economy ceases; trade routes dissolve |
| **Belief** | Faith networks; ideologues; population piety | Magic/technology; cults; reality maintenance | Material output decays; events become surreal |
| **Influence** | Covert operations; prestige; alliances | Espionage; faction politics; court appointments | Diplomatic range shrinks; heroes defect |
| **Entropy** | Unresolved crises; failed policies; high Paperwork | Nothing; entropy only decays through stability actions | Cascade events; collapse; Dominion dissolution |
| **Paperwork** | Every action; every decree; every inquiry | Bureaucratic Reform tech; efficient advisors | Administrative paralysis; decrees go unread |

## 2.2 Regional Material Outputs

Regions produce physical outputs that feed Dominion-level resources. These are secondary to the ideological economy but necessary infrastructure.

| Output | Produces | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Agricultural Output** | Belief (population is fed and content) | Collapses if population loses faith in harvests |
| **Industrial Output** | Trust (goods = promises kept) | High unrest cost; requires labour |
| **Trade Volume** | Trust + Influence | Requires stable trade routes and at least two non-hostile neighbours |
| **Scholarly Output** | Research points (feed tech tree) | Requires educated population; scholars are politically inconvenient |
| **Guild Revenue** | Influence | Guilds operate semi-autonomously; their loyalty is expensive to maintain |
| **Cult Tithes** | Belief + Entropy | High Belief return; proportionally high Entropy generation |

## 2.3 Raw Deposits (Strategic Resources)

Rare physical resources that unlock specific buildings, units, or tech branches. Not the primary economy — but contested.

| Deposit | Unlocks | Conflict likelihood |
|---|---|---|
| Resonance Ore | Belief amplification structures | High |
| Entropic Minerals | Entropy weaponisation; forbidden tech | Extreme |
| Archival Stone | Extended Paperwork capacity; bureaucratic monuments | Low (until someone claims it matters) |
| Deep Water | Population growth; agricultural boom | Moderate |
| Forgotten Metal | Advanced military units | High |
| Void Salt | Cult founding bonus; reality stabilisation | Variable (depends on who discovers it first) |

// Raw deposits are revealed by the `Survey Resources` mission. Un-surveyed deposits exist but generate no output.

# 3. POPULATION MODEL

## 3.1 Population Strata

Each region's population is divided into strata. Each stratum has its own Loyalty, Belief Composition, and Vice Profile.

| Stratum | Role | Generates | Destabilises when |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Commons** | Labour, agricultural base | Agricultural Output | Hungry, over-taxed, or theologically confused |
| **Guild Members** | Skilled labour, trade | Guild Revenue, Trade Volume | Monopoly broken or guild charter revoked |
| **Bureaucrats** | Administration, Paperwork processing | Paperwork reduction (or increase) | Underemployed, or given too much authority |
| **Cult Members** | Ideological maintenance | Belief, Cult Tithes | Heresy detected; rival cult funded |
| **Elites** | Stability, Influence generation | Influence | Their conspiracies go unregistered |
| **Dissidents** | Entropy generation | Entropy | They exist |
| **Heroes** | Direct action; mission execution | Heroic Layer output | Ignored, under-resourced, or too successful |

## 3.2 Population Attributes

Each stratum in each region is tracked across these dimensions:

- **Loyalty** (0–100): Loyalty to the current Dominion controller. Below 20, defection events fire.
- **Belief Composition**: Which ideology the stratum follows (can differ from official regional ideology).
- **Vice Profile**: Accumulated behavioural conditions (*e.g., "Drunken Productivity," "Generational Paranoia," "Excessive Audit Enthusiasm"*). Vices emerge from long-term events and modify stratum output.
- **Education Level**: Affects Research output and ideology susceptibility.
- **Growth Rate**: Positive or negative; modified by food, disease events, and belief in the future.

// Population Belief Composition drifts independently of governance. A Syndicate-governed region
// can develop a thriving secret cult. A Federation of Belief can become practically atheist.
// The gap between official ideology and actual belief is where events live.

# 4. REGION ATTRIBUTES

Every region in the simulation carries these fields in its JSON state:

| Field | Type | Description |
|---|---|---|
| `id` | string | Unique identifier (e.g., `"the_pale_midlands"`) |
| `name` | string | Display name |
| `archetype` | enum | `federation_of_belief`, `syndicate_of_necessity`, `realm_of_regret` |
| `controller` | string | Faction ID currently in control |
| `stability` | int (0–100) | Below 30: unrest events. Below 10: revolt. |
| `entropy` | int (0–100) | Accumulated instability. Above 75: crisis event threshold. |
| `belief_composition` | object | `{ideology_id: share}` — must sum to 1.0 |
| `dominant_ideology` | string | Ideology with >50% share (or `contested`) |
| `infrastructure_level` | int (0–10) | Gates missions and policy effectiveness |
| `resources` | object | Material output rates and deposit list |
| `population` | object | Per-stratum loyalty, vice, growth |
| `active_policies` | list | Currently running policies (from missions.md §2.2) |
| `missions_completed` | list | Claimed missions scoped to this region |
| `events_active` | list | Ongoing event chains |
| `type` | enum | `core_homeland`, `colony`, `occupied_territory`, `subrealm`, `digital_enclave` |

# 5. REGION TYPES

Region type modifies base costs, event tables, and mission availability. Defined in missions.md §11 but expanded here.

## 5.1 Core Homeland

The Dominion's founding territory. The administrative and ideological heartland.

- All policy costs −10%
- Stability recovery rate ×1.5
- Entropy decay rate ×1.5
- Failure events less severe (bureaucracy absorbs shocks)
- Belief Composition more resistant to external propaganda
- **Cannot be abandoned** without triggering a Dominion Collapse event

## 5.2 Colony

Administered territory, acquired through expansion. Loyal in paperwork; ambivalent in practice.

- Infrastructure requirements for missions −2
- Unrest events more frequent
- Loyalty decays passively unless Influence is spent
- High population growth (they are building something and have not yet realised it is futile)
- Eligible for Core Integration mission (converts to Core Homeland after sustained stability)

## 5.3 Occupied Territory

Taken by force; governed by necessity.

- Military missions available immediately, no prerequisites
- Diplomacy missions blocked (no one trusts you here yet)
- High passive Entropy generation
- Loyalty starts at 10; declines without active garrison and propaganda
- Belief Composition rapidly drifts away from Dominion ideology
- Can be released as a Protectorate (installs puppet; reduces Entropy, reduces control)

## 5.4 Subrealm

Underground domains — cavern systems, forgotten bureaucratic archives, and faith-based economies operating on entirely different axioms.

- Surface missions unavailable
- Unique Subrealm mission branch unlocked (see §8.2)
- Population: Dwarfs, Software Daemons, Archive Priests (distinct strata from surface types)
- Economy runs on **Archival Trust** (a secondary Trust-variant; convertible but lossy)
- Entropy behaves differently: Subrealms accumulate structural decay, not political instability
- Connecting a Subrealm to the surface costs significant Paperwork; the forms have not been updated since the last era

## 5.5 Digital Enclave

Regions that have partially or fully migrated to a digital substrate. Reality is maintained by consensus and server uptime.

- Belief functions as literal computation substrate (Belief collapse = server failure)
- Population consists of Software Daemons and digitised personalities
- Immune to conventional military action; vulnerable to ideological hacking missions
- High Research output; extremely high Entropy when destabilised
- Unlocked via `Theoretical Digitisation` tech (Forbidden Knowledge branch)

# 7. REGION MECHANICS

## 7.1 Stability

Stability (0–100) represents how successfully a region maintains the fiction that things are under control.

| Stability range | State | Events |
|---|---|---|
| 75–100 | Stable | Occasional prosperity events; rare crises |
| 50–74 | Managed | Standard event frequency; mild unrest possible |
| 30–49 | Strained | Unrest events likely; Entropy accumulates faster |
| 10–29 | Unstable | Revolt events; Belief drift accelerates; Loyalty drops |
| 0–9 | Collapsing | Revolt fires automatically; region may change controller |

**Stability modifiers:**
- Active garrisons: +5/turn (if loyal)
- Funded propaganda: +3/turn (while active)
- Unfulfilled promises: −3/turn (per outstanding decree)
- High Entropy: −1/turn per 10 Entropy above 50
- Elite Loyalty > 70: +2/turn
- Dissident stratum > 20% population share: −5/turn

## 7.2 Entropy Accumulation

Entropy (0–100) measures systemic pressure. It builds silently and releases catastrophically.

**Entropy sources:**
- Every active Cult: +2/turn
- Active covert missions targeting the region: +1–4/turn
- High Paperwork backlog: +1/turn per overflow tier
- Occupied Territory status: +3/turn baseline
- Failed policies: +5 on_cancel
- Dissident stratum events: +3–8 per event

**Entropy decay:**
- Stability actions (direct investment): −5 to −15 per action
- Bureaucratic Reform tech: passive decay bonus
- Grand Auditor advisor: −2/turn in Core Homeland
- Belief Composition > 70% unified ideology: −1/turn

**Entropy thresholds:**
- 50+: Crisis events begin rolling
- 75+: Entropy Alarm fires in HUD; severe events possible
- 90+: Cascade event guaranteed within 1–3 turns
- 100: Collapse event; region enters Contested status

## 7.3 Belief Drift

Belief Composition changes each turn based on:

- Propaganda campaigns (push toward target ideology)
- Hero missions (Propagandise, Recruit Cultists, Found Cult)
- Neighbouring region ideology (passive contagion)
- Elite and Bureaucrat loyalty (high loyalty stabilises composition)
- Events (theological crises, miracles, god bankruptcies)

A region where Belief Composition diverges more than 40 points from the controlling faction's ideology begins generating passive Entropy and Loyalty decay, regardless of military presence.

// This is intentional. You cannot hold a region by force alone indefinitely.
// The simulation believes in ideology more than it believes in armies.

## 7.4 Ideology Drift Events

| Trigger | Event example |
|---|---|
| Belief composition shifts >20 in one turn | *"A new theology has emerged. Its origins are unclear. Its tax implications are worse."* |
| Dominant ideology becomes `contested` | *"The Ministry of Belief has issued a clarification that clarifies nothing."* |
| A cult exceeds 30% population share | *"The Cult of [X] requests official recognition and a dedicated postal code."* |
| All strata loyalty > 80 | *"Region [X] reports unprecedented stability. Investigators have been dispatched."* |

# 8. SPECIAL REGIONAL MECHANICS

## 8.1 Trade Routes

Trade routes connect adjacent regions and generate Trust and Influence for both parties.

- Require: both regions Stability > 30 and not at war
- Disrupted by: war, covert sabotage, Entropy > 75, trade dispute event
- Enhanced by: Trade Hub mission, Merchant Guilds mission, Guild Revenue high
- Each active trade route generates a small Paperwork cost (forms, tariffs, complaints)

## 8.2 Subrealm Access

Connecting a surface region to an adjacent Subrealm is a multi-mission process:

1. **Survey the Depth** — reveal the Subrealm and its resource deposits
2. **Negotiate Access Rights** — file Form SR-4/A with the Under-Bureau (requires Paperwork investment)
3. **Establish Surface Link** — build a connection infrastructure (turns and Trust)
4. **Integrate (Optional)** — convert the Subrealm into a Colony (very high Paperwork; high unrest among Subrealm population)

Subrealms that are accessed but not integrated generate Archival Trust independently; players receive a share based on influence penetration.

## 8.3 Regional Events

Each region rolls for events each turn based on its state. Event tables are weighted by:

- Current stability tier
- Entropy level
- Belief Composition
- Active policies and missions
- Population vice profiles

**Example events by category:**

| Category | Example |
|---|---|
| Economy | *"Guild refuses to honour contracts pending theological clarification."* |
| Ideology | *"A minor god declares the region their exclusive jurisdiction. Paperwork dispute ongoing."* |
| Military | *"Garrison has developed a collective superstition about Tuesdays. Combat effectiveness variable."* |
| Diplomatic | *"Adjacent region has filed a formal complaint about your propaganda. They request a rebuttal in triplicate."* |
| Collapse | *"The regional tax authority has seceded from the region."* |
| Rare | *"A utopia has spontaneously emerged. Investigators suspect fraud."* |

## 8.4 Regional Collapse

When a region's Stability hits 0, a Collapse event fires:

1. Controller loses administrative control
2. Region enters **Contested** status for 1–3 turns
3. Each faction with Influence > 20 in the region contests control
4. Highest combined Influence + active Hero presence wins
5. The new controller inherits all active events, debts, and vice profiles

// Collapse is not always bad for the Dominion causing it.
// A destabilised rival's region creates an opportunity for Influence projection.

# 9. REGION DIFFERENTIATION

Each region has a unique modifier profile derived from its lore, resource mix, and starting conditions. These modifiers are set at world generation and persist unless overwritten by missions.

**Examples:**

| Region | Unique modifier | Source |
|---|---|---|
| The Verification Plateau | `+15% Belief from elections; −10 Stability per theological candidate scandal` | Federation of Belief with divine democracy |

| The Northern Reaches | `Resource deposits ×2; infrastructure build cost ×1.5; garrison supply cost ×1.5` | Resource-rich, infrastructure-hostile |

# 10. DESIGN PRINCIPLES

- **Belief is the real economy.** Material resources feed Dominion operations, but Belief is what keeps the simulation coherent. Regions that lose Belief do not just become unproductive — their material output becomes uncertain.
- **Every region is a distinct problem.** No two regions should respond to the same policy identically. Unique modifiers and different population strata compositions mean solutions do not transfer.
- **Entropy is irreversible without active effort.** It will not decay on its own. Players who ignore entropy accumulation will eventually pay for it at the worst possible time.
- **Stability can be faked.** A region at Stability 60 with Entropy 80 looks fine on the surface. The simulation knows better. UI should not hide this but should require the player to look for it.
- **Subrealms are optional depth.** They should reward players who engage with them but never block core progression for players who ignore them.
- **Satirical tone in all events.** Region events should read like dispatches from a tired, underfunded Ministry that has seen everything before and is no longer surprised.
